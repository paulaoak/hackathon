Experimental Procedure:

Materials and Reagents:

  • Cyclohexene: 12.3 mL
  • Bromine: 6.24 mL
  • Solvent: Carbon tetrachloride (CCl₄) or chloroform (CHCl₃)
  • Ice bath
  • Drying agent (e.g., anhydrous magnesium sulfate)
  • Silica gel for purification

Apparatus:

  • Round-bottom flask (250 mL)
  • Magnetic stirrer and stir bar
  • Ice bath
  • Dropping funnel
  • Separation funnel
  • Rotary evaporator
  • Column chromatography setup

Procedure:

  1. Preparation:
    • Place a 250 mL round-bottom flask in an ice bath.
    • Add 12.3 mL of cyclohexene to the flask.
  2. Bromination Reaction:
    • Slowly add 6.24 mL of bromine dissolved in 20 mL of carbon tetrachloride or chloroform to the cyclohexene solution while stirring. Use a dropping funnel to control the addition and keep the reaction mixture at 0-5°C.
    • Stir the mixture continuously for 1-2 hours, monitoring the reaction progress.
  3. Isolation of the Product:
    • After the reaction is complete, wash the mixture with water to remove residual bromine.
    • Separate the organic layer using a separation funnel.
    • Dry the organic layer over anhydrous magnesium sulfate.
    • Filter the drying agent and concentrate the solution using a rotary evaporator.
  4. Purification:
    • Purify the crude product by column chromatography on silica gel. Use a suitable solvent system (e.g., hexane/ethyl acetate) to elute the desired product.
    • Collect the fractions containing trans-1,4-dibromocyclohexane and remove the solvent under reduced pressure.
  5. Characterization:
    • Confirm the structure and purity of the product using NMR spectroscopy, IR spectroscopy, and mass spectrometry.

Reference:

For further details, refer to the original paper: "Stereochemistry and Mechanism of the Bromination of Cyclohexene" published in the Journal of Organic Chemistry.
